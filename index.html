League Structure

  • LFA Premier League
    • 16 member clubs competing in a home/away format
    • 38 matchdays across the full season
    • Champions qualify for CAF Champions League
    • Bottom 2 clubs relegated to First Division
  • LFA First Division
    • 14 clubs competing for promotion
    • Top 2 clubs promoted to Premier League
  • Liberian FA Cup
    • Open to all registered LFA clubs
    • Single elimination format from Round of 32
    • Final hosted at SKD Sports Complex
  • CAF Competitions
    • Champions League — Premier League champion entry
    • Confederation Cup — FA Cup winner entry
  • Youth Leagues
    • U-17 National League
    • U-20 Development League
    • Women's Premier Football League (WPFL)

Club Regulations

  • Player Registration
    • Maximum 25 players on senior squad list
    • Minimum 8 homegrown players required
    • Foreign player limit: 5 per matchday squad
    • Transfer windows: January and July
  • Stadium Requirements
    • Minimum capacity of 5,000 seats
    • Floodlighting mandatory for evening fixtures
    • Medical room and doping control facilities
  • Financial Fair Play
    • Annual accounts submitted by March 31
    • Wage-to-revenue ratio cap of 70%
    • No outstanding debt to players or staff
  • Disciplinary
    • Yellow card accumulation: 5 = 1 match ban
    • Red card automatic 3-match suspension
    • Appeals heard within 7 days of ruling
  • Anti-Doping
    • CAF and WADA protocols enforced
    • Random testing throughout the season
    • Mandatory player education program

Since 1936

The Liberia Football Association (LFA) is the governing body of football in Liberia, founded in 1936 and affiliated with both the Confederation of African Football (CAF) and FIFA. For nearly nine decades, the LFA has been the institutional backbone of the sport in the nation, nurturing generations of talented players who have gone on to represent Liberia on the continental and world stages.

The LFA Premier League, the highest tier of club football in Liberia, has produced iconic clubs like Mighty Barrolle, Barrack Young Controllers, and Monrovia Club Breweries — clubs that carry the passion and identity of their communities into every match. Their rivalries are the stuff of legend, and their history is woven into the fabric of Liberian culture.

The Association operates under a reformed governance framework introduced in 2018, bringing modern standards of financial accountability, youth development, and anti-doping compliance to Liberian football. Today, the LFA oversees the Premier League, First Division, FA Cup, Women's Premier Football League, and national youth competitions.

On the international stage, Liberia has produced footballing legends including George Weah — the only African to win the FIFA World Player of the Year award — whose legacy continues to inspire the generation of players now competing in the LFA Premier League every week.

The LFA's long-term vision is to develop Liberian football infrastructure, qualify for the FIFA World Cup, and create sustainable pathways from grassroots academies to professional clubs. With continued investment, dedicated coaches, and the fire of the Liberian football spirit, the beautiful game in this nation is only growing stronger.
